# ContentCam Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2 May 2026_

ContentCam is built by an Australian solo developer for content creators who
shoot both portrait and landscape video. The app is designed to collect no
personal data, send no analytics, and require no account.

## Data we collect

**None.** The app does not request, transmit, or store any personally
identifiable information. There is no account, no login, no telemetry,
no third-party SDKs.

## Permissions

ContentCam requests three iOS permissions, all required for its core function:

- **Camera** — to record video.
- **Microphone** — to record audio with your video.
- **Photo Library (add only)** — to save your recorded videos to your Photos
  library. The app does not request read access to your existing photo
  library; it can only add new videos that you record in the app.

All three are explained in iOS's permission prompts. You can revoke them at
any time in iOS Settings → ContentCam.

## Data we don't collect

- We do not track you across apps or websites.
- We do not collect your location, contacts, calendar, browsing history,
  or any other personal data.
- We do not have access to your existing photo library — only the ability
  to add new videos that you record in the app.

## Where your videos are stored

- Inside the app's sandboxed Documents directory until you delete them or
  uninstall the app.
- In your iOS Photos library when you choose to save a take from the
  in-app review screen. ContentCam does not delete videos from your Photos
  library; that's managed by you in the iOS Photos app.

## Third-party services

None. ContentCam does not use any third-party SDKs, analytics services,
ad networks, or backend servers. It is a fully on-device app.

## Children

ContentCam is rated 4+ on the App Store and is suitable for general
audiences. We do not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including
children.

## Contact

For questions about this policy, email **contentcam.app@gmail.com**.

## Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy (e.g., adding cloud sync or accounts in a
future version), we'll update this page and notify users via App Store
release notes.
